Ayurveda is an ancient system of medicine from India, focusing on balance between mind, body, and spirit for overall well-being.
It emphasizes natural remedies, including herbs, diet, and lifestyle changes tailored to individual constitutions or "doshas."
Rooted in holistic health, Ayurveda promotes prevention and long-term wellness through harmony with nature.

Know About Ayurveda

Ayurveda, an ancient system of natural healing, originated in India over 5,000 years ago and is rooted in the belief that health and wellness depend on a delicate balance between the mind, body, and spirit. It focuses on promoting good health through natural therapies and lifestyle changes, rather than treating diseases alone. Ayurveda identifies three life energies, or "doshas"—Vata, Pitta, and Kapha—which govern an individual's physical and mental constitution.

Ayurvedic medicine emphasizes the use of natural herbs like turmeric, ashwagandha, and tulsi, along with holistic practices such as oil massages (Abhyanga) and detoxification therapies (Panchakarma). Ayurveda is also preventative in nature, encouraging a lifestyle that aligns with nature's cycles to enhance longevity and vitality, making it a timeless and integrative system of healthcare.

Herbs in Ayurveda

Tulsi

-Botanical Name:Ocimum sanctum.
-Common Name:Tulsi, Holy Basil.
-Habitat:Tropical and subtropical regions, commonly grown in homes and gardens in India.
-Uses:Respiratory disorders, fever, stress, immune booster.
-Disease and Cure:Treats coughs, colds, bronchitis, and asthma.

Neem

-Botanical Name:Azadirachta indica.
-Common Name:Neem, Indian Lilac.
-Habitat:Indian subcontinent, tropical and semi-tropical regions.
-Uses:Anti-inflammatory, antifungal, antibacterial.
-Disease and Cure:Skin conditions like eczema, acne, psoriasis; dental care.
