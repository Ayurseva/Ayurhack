About AYUSH

"आरोग्यं परमं भाग्यं स्वास्थ्यं सर्वार्थसाधनम्॥"

(Health is the biggest wealth in one's life)

AYUSH, an acronym for Ayurveda, Yoga and Naturopathy, Unani, Siddha, and Homeopathy, represents the traditional systems of medicine practiced in India. The AYUSH Ministry, established by the Government of India, aims to promote these ancient healthcare practices alongside modern medicine. Ayurveda, a system based on the balance of bodily energies, emphasizes holistic health through diet, herbal treatments, and lifestyle changes.

Yoga and Naturopathy focus on physical postures, meditation, and natural healing processes, while Unani and Siddha are ancient systems rooted in the Greco-Arabic and Tamil traditions, respectively, utilizing herbal remedies and natural therapies. Homeopathy, founded on the principle of "like cures like," uses highly diluted substances to trigger the body’s healing responses.

Practices

Ayurveda

Ayurveda is an ancient system of natural healing that originated in India over 5,000 years ago, focusing on balancing the body, mind, and spirit through diet, lifestyle, and herbal treatments. It emphasizes the use of natural remedies and holistic practices to promote health and well-being.

Yoga

Yoga is a physical, mental, and spiritual practice that originated in ancient India, focusing on breath control, meditation, and postures to enhance flexibility and inner peace. It aims to promote holistic well-being by connecting the body, mind, and spirit.

Unani

Unani is a traditional system of medicine that originated in ancient Greece and was further developed in the Islamic world, emphasizing balance in the body's humors (blood, phlegm, yellow bile, and black bile) for health. It combines natural remedies, dietary practices, and lifestyle adjustments for holistic healing.

Sidda

Siddha is an ancient system of medicine that originated in South India, focusing on spiritual healing, alchemy, and the balance of the body's elements. It uses herbs, minerals, and lifestyle practices to promote health and longevity, emphasizing prevention and holistic well-being.

Homeopathy

Homeopathy is a natural system of medicine founded in the 18th century by Samuel Hahnemann, based on the principle of "like cures like," where substances that cause symptoms in large doses are used in small, diluted amounts to treat similar symptoms. It aims to stimulate the body's self-healing response using highly diluted remedies.
